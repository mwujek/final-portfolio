Project Info

During my last semester at University of San Francisco, I enrolled in an elective course in Data Visualization taught by the accomplished and code artist, Scott Murray. For one of our projects, we were asked to find interesting data related to the bay area. After analyzing the data, Scott asked us to create a visualization that told a story about our observations. During my first few years at USF, I used public transportation exclusively to travel everywhere in the city. I quickly discovered that during certain hours, some bus lines were painfully slow. I decided that looking into the bus schedules could reveal a lot of interesting information; therefore, I grabbed the SFMTA bus schedule on SF OpenData and found that many lines had integrated delays during peak hours. Even with sophisticated data tools like Tableau, it was difficult to compare and contrast the thousands of daily trips over the most popular routes. I complied all of the data and made an interactive visualization that shows the differences in route durations throughout the any given weekday. Rather than animate each trip relative to the departure time, I decided to animate them all at once so you can instantly see the range and variance in the trip duration of each route. In conclusion, I believe this project was an interesting data exploration and I look forward to publishing my latest D3 project with my favorite shared transportation company, Scoot Networks.
